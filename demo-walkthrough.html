Box Round 1 - Sr PM, AI Agents
30-minute virtual panel walkthrough

Move from meeting Q&A to governed work orchestration.

This page is the presenter cockpit for the final Box case walkthrough. It gives the panel the arc, the live demo beats, the evidence to point at, and the platform primitives underneath the Acme scenario.

One thing to remember: Acme is the scenario. The product is the reusable primitive set underneath it: permission-aware context, deterministic checks, reviewed actions, lifecycle events, audit, revalidation, and governed records.
Recommendation

Expand the agent's job

Today's agent summarizes meetings. The proposed agent helps a regulated team move from discussion to a defensible decision and routed follow-through.

Feature pillars

Three visible surfaces

Decision Brief, Agent Actions, and Governed Record. Each surface maps to a platform primitive, not a one-off Acme script.

Business framing

Time, confidence, control

Reduce decision-to-ready time, increase confidence in evidence and approvals, and make every agent action reviewable, permission-aware, and auditable.

BRIEF

What the walkthrough covers

The first two minutes state the user, the problem, the recommendation, and the demo path. The rest of the walkthrough proves the product pattern through one regulated workflow.

Entry point

Meeting rail / chat

Dana invokes the agent from the meeting context while her Acme credit memo draft is on screen.

Happy path 1

Decision Brief generation

The agent turns meeting context, linked content, metadata, workflow state, and permissions into typed readiness rows.

Happy path 2

Approval/action routing

Rows are staged, validated in Agent Actions, executed through approval gates, and revalidated until the brief is approval-ready.

Edge cases

Controlled failures

Blocked mutation, restricted legal source, missing evidence, source conflict, and stale governed record.

Close

Roadmap and metrics

The close covers phased roadmap, KPIs, risks, mitigations, and the Q&A bridge.

Technical hedge

What is prototype-local

In this prototype, the Acme loop uses API-local lifecycle events and visible simulated responses. The design invariant is the important part: event, recompute, stage, validate, execute, audit, and revalidate. A general dispatcher and persistent lifecycle store are platform roadmap items.

30:00

Time plan

Use the time to reduce cognitive load first, then prove the hard part live. Optional proof stays optional.

Q&A

Deep-dive bridge

Invite the panel to choose a lane: Product, Engineering, or Design. Keep answers tied to the primitive set.

Is this just document Q&A with a nicer package?
No. Document Q&A grounds an answer. This product computes readiness, stages remediations, validates actions, records lifecycle events, and seals a work product that can later be checked for freshness.
What is real versus simulated in the prototype?
The Acme loop uses API-local lifecycle events and visible simulated responses for Credit, Legal, and Priya. The invariant being proven is the product boundary: event, recompute, stage, validate, execute, audit, and revalidate.
How would this generalize beyond credit?
Swap the recipe, allowed sources, policy artifact, approval matrix, dependency graph, work-product contract, revalidation rules, and eval pack. The surface pattern remains permission-aware context, typed readiness, reviewed actions, lifecycle events, and governed records.
Close in one sentence: The model is one component; the governed orchestration substrate is the product.
