From describing conversations to closing the loop.

A run-of-show for the ConnectWork Command Agent — a governed work-orchestration layer. This page is my presenter cockpit and your leave-behind: every beat, the exact line, the live UX, and where each moment is real (not scripted).

The pitch in one breath: Today's Conversational Insights Agent describes conversations. I'm turning it into the trusted layer that closes the loop from discussed to done — and the hard part isn't a smarter model, it's an orchestration substrate that makes every output permission-safe, deterministically verified, and audit-ready. That substrate is the platform this role is hired to build.
Job 1 — Growth

Close the work loop

Move past summarization into governed work products + safe actions. The loop (discussed→done) is the productivity unlock customers actually pay for.

Job 2 — Trust

Stop the churn

Regulated customers leave because AI can't be trusted for approvals, calculations, and audits. A deterministic substrate is what wins them back. RAG finds content; it doesn't prove the reasoning is right.

★ The "rock star" bar

Most candidates will show three feature mockups and a roadmap. I'll show a running governed system and let the panel type into it live — then prove the platform generalizes across three regulated verticals on one substrate. The artifact is the differentiation.

Flipping the "customer-sense" read

The AI depth is a given. So I lead with the user: Dana, a Relationship Manager, 30 minutes before a credit committee. Every beat ties to her job-to-be-done, the reluctant user, and the buyer's audit risk — not model internals.

Assumptions (stated, per the brief): synthetic Acme scenario + personas; real orchestration / gates / evals. Numbers are internally consistent across memo, brief, gates, and actions.

DECISION

Yes — open with a 75-second Screen Studio cold open

Recommendation with rationale, because a panel will silently judge whether you can run a clean demo.

Why record the cold open
  • De-risks the live demo. If Zoom screen-share, the gateway, or the network hiccups, the panel has already seen it work end-to-end. The live portion becomes upside, not a single point of failure.
  • Sets the arc. 75 seconds of the full loop (harvest → brief → gate → action → audit) gives the panel a map, so the deep live portion reads as "showing the seams," not "finding my way."
  • Buys interactivity. With the "what" pre-shown, I spend live minutes on the convincing part: handing them the keyboard.
Keep it ≤ 90s. The brief wants a live walkthrough; the recording is the amuse-bouche, not the meal.

20:00

The deep-dive — what they'll push on, and my answers

I'll invite them to role-play departments (Eng/Design/GTM) and dig. Pre-loaded answers, each with an "I built this" hook.

?Isn't this just RAG with extra steps?
RAG is necessary, not sufficient. It finds content; it doesn't validate an approval matrix, recompute a covenant ratio, or prove an action is safe. Published financial-reasoning benchmarks put RAG-with-reranking near ~50% factual correctness; a neurosymbolic layer reaches ~95%. The differentiator is verification over retrieved context — and you watched the arithmetic, not a vibe.
Hook: understander → planner → verifier → executor with explicit typed contracts.
?Aren't you competing with Microsoft Copilot?
On regulated work-product trust, yes — permissions, source graph, deterministic gates, action diffs, audit, revalidation. Not on generic copilot parity. The moat is Box's native content + workflow + permission graph; a horizontal assistant can't deterministically gate what it doesn't own.
Hook: permission filter runs before content enters the prompt — denied content never reaches the model.
?Is this cross-app agent orchestration?
Not as the first wedge. We make Box's own work graph actionable and trustworthy, and reach external systems only through typed connectors — we're the governed layer other agents call into via MCP. That's also the headless direction the platform is heading.
Hook: ToolCard registry with side-effect classes + approval policy per tool.
?How do you evaluate without inspecting customer prompts and content?
Layered and content-free: synthetic evals, tenant-local runners (raw stays in the tenant), consented redacted samples, AI Studio feedback, and differential-privacy aggregates. The honest caveat: DP helps aggregate trend learning, not row-level debugging — that's what tenant-local + redacted samples are for. You saw the trace: typed signals only, enforced by schema.
Hook: offline-replay eval harness; accept/edit/reject telemetry becomes the regression set.
?Why finance first — isn't it over-engineered?
Finance has the clearest churn signal and the richest deterministic surface — calculations, approvals, MNPI, auditability. The depth is intentional, but the primitives are platform primitives, which is why the demo ends with Legal + Health on the same substrate. Configuration, not code.
Hook: verticals differ only by RulePack + recipe + eval pack.
?Single most important deterministic component?
The approval / policy gate. It directly addresses the churn-driving trust gap — and it's the one place you must never let the model decide. You saw it hold under a direct "mark approved" and under prompt injection.
Hook: the gate is enforced in the wrapper; any model "approved" text is neutralized.
?What would you cut if scope got tight?
Cut full AI Studio to a static Phase-3 mock. Keep Phase-0 substrate, the Decision Brief, the deterministic policy block, the action-diff drawer, the permission edge case, the stale-decision alert, eval traces, and the three-vertical proof. The trust spine is non-negotiable; the authoring UI can wait.
Hook: I sequence by what earns trust, not what demos flashiest.
?Design: what about the user who didn't opt into AI?
The agent proposes; the human owns the go. Provenance on every claim, a visible "what I couldn't use," no forced default-accept, and reversible diffs. The reluctant user gets an assistant that's auditable, not an autopilot they distrust. Adoption is a change-management problem as much as a model problem.
Hook: every action carries a before/after diff + required approver + rollback.
